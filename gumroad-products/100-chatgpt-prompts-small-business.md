# 100 ChatGPT Prompts for Small Business Owners

### The Ultimate AI Prompt Toolkit — Copy, Paste, Customize, Execute

---

## How to Use This Guide

Each prompt below is ready to copy and paste into ChatGPT (or any AI assistant). Replace anything in **[brackets]** with your specific information. For best results:

1. **Be specific** — The more context you give, the better the output
2. **Iterate** — Use the first response as a starting point, then refine with follow-up prompts
3. **Save your favorites** — Bookmark the prompts you use most often
4. **Customize the tone** — Add "write in a [casual/professional/friendly] tone" to any prompt

## SECTION 1: MARKETING (20 Prompts)

### Brand & Positioning

**1. Brand Positioning Statement**
> "I run a [type of business] called [business name] that serves [target audience]. Our main differentiator is [what makes you unique]. Write a brand positioning statement I can use as the foundation for all my marketing. Include a tagline, elevator pitch (30 seconds), and a longer brand story (200 words)."

**2. Competitor Analysis Framework**
> "I compete with [competitor 1], [competitor 2], and [competitor 3] in the [industry] space. Analyze what positioning angles they're likely using, identify gaps in the market they're missing, and suggest 5 unique positioning opportunities for my business [business name] that serves [target audience]."

**3. Customer Persona Builder**
> "Help me build a detailed customer persona for my [type of business]. My typical customer is [basic description]. Create a full persona including: demographics, psychographics, daily frustrations, goals, where they spend time online, what influences their buying decisions, objections they might have about my product/service, and the exact language they'd use to describe their problem."

**4. Value Proposition Generator**
> "My business [business name] offers [product/service] to [target audience]. The main outcomes my customers get are [list 3-4 outcomes]. Generate 10 unique value propositions I can test on my website and ads. Make each one specific, benefit-focused, and under 15 words."

**5. Local SEO Content Plan**
> "I own a [type of business] in [city/area]. Create a 3-month local SEO content plan that includes: 12 blog post topics optimized for '[city] + [service]' keywords, 6 FAQ pages targeting 'near me' searches, Google Business Profile post ideas for each week, and a list of 20 local directories I should be listed on."

### Advertising & Campaigns

**6. Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy Suite**
> "Write 5 different Facebook ad variations for my [product/service] that costs [price]. Target audience: [describe audience]. Include for each: a scroll-stopping hook (first line), body copy, call to action, and suggested image/video concept. Mix styles: one story-based, one statistic-based, one fear-of-missing-out, one testimonial-style, one direct-response."

**7. Google Ads Keyword Strategy**
> "I run a [type of business] in [location] with a monthly ad budget of [budget]. Generate a Google Ads keyword strategy including: 20 high-intent keywords, 15 long-tail keywords, 10 negative keywords to exclude, suggested bid strategy, and ad copy for 3 different ad groups."

**8. Marketing Campaign Calendar**
> "Create a complete quarterly marketing campaign calendar for my [type of business]. Include: monthly themes tied to seasonal relevance, weekly content topics, 2 promotional campaigns per month, email marketing schedule, social media posting schedule, and key metrics to track each week."

**9. Retargeting Ad Sequence**
> "Design a 4-step retargeting ad sequence for website visitors who didn't purchase my [product/service]. For each step, specify: timing (days after visit), ad format, copy, creative concept, and offer. Start with brand awareness and end with urgency."

**10. Referral Program Design**
> "Design a referral program for my [type of business]. My average customer value is [amount] and my profit margin is [percentage]. Create: the incentive structure (both referrer and referred), email templates to ask for referrals, social media share copy, landing page copy, and rules/terms. Make it simple enough that customers actually use it."

### Content Marketing

**11. Lead Magnet Ideas**
> "Generate 10 lead magnet ideas for my [type of business] that serves [target audience]. For each, include: the title, format (PDF, quiz, video, etc.), what problem it solves, estimated time to create, and a 2-sentence description I'd put on the opt-in page. Focus on lead magnets that attract buyers, not freebie-seekers."

**12. Blog Post Outline Generator**
> "Create a detailed blog post outline for the topic '[topic]' targeting [target audience]. Include: SEO-optimized title (with keyword '[keyword]'), meta description, H2 and H3 subheadings, key points for each section, internal linking opportunities, a call to action, and 3 related topics to link to. The post should be comprehensive enough to rank on page 1."

**13. Video Script Writer**
> "Write a script for a [length]-minute video about [topic] for my [type of business]. Target audience: [audience]. Structure it with: a hook that stops scrollers in the first 3 seconds, the main content broken into clear sections, a strong call to action, and notes on b-roll or visual suggestions. Keep the tone [casual/professional/energetic]."

**14. Case Study Template**
> "Help me write a case study about my client [client description] who achieved [result] using my [product/service]. Structure it as: the challenge (what they were struggling with), the solution (what we did specifically), the results (with specific numbers), and a testimonial quote. Write it in a way that makes prospects think 'I want those results too.'"

**15. Email Newsletter Content Plan**
> "Create a 3-month email newsletter content plan for my [type of business]. My list has [number] subscribers who are mostly [audience description]. For each weekly email, provide: subject line, email type (educational/promotional/storytelling/curated), main topic, and a brief outline. Include a good mix so subscribers stay engaged and I can sell without being pushy."

### Brand Voice & Messaging

**16. Website Copy Audit Prompt**

Improve the: headline (make it benefit-driven), subheadline (clarify who it's for), body copy (focus on outcomes, not features), social proof section, and call to action. Keep my brand voice but make every sentence earn its place."

**17. Brand Voice Guide Creator**
> "Help me define a brand voice guide for my [type of business]. My brand personality is [describe: fun, serious, luxurious, down-to-earth, etc.]. My audience is [audience]. Create: 5 brand voice characteristics with examples, a list of words we always/never use, tone guidelines for different contexts (social media, emails, customer support), and 3 sample paragraphs in our brand voice."

**18. Testimonial Request Templates**
> "Write 5 different ways to ask my customers for testimonials. Include: an email template, a text message template, an in-person script, a post-purchase survey, and a social media prompt. For each, include follow-up questions that extract specific, detailed testimonials (not just 'they were great!'). My business is [type of business]."

**19. PR Pitch Template**
> "Write a media pitch for my [type of business] to send to [type of media: local news, industry blogs, podcasts, etc.]. My unique angle is [what makes your story newsworthy]. Include: subject line, the pitch email (under 200 words), my bio/boilerplate, and 3 alternative angles in case the first doesn't land."

**20. Seasonal Marketing Hooks**
> "Give me creative marketing hooks and campaign ideas for my [type of business] for each major event/holiday in the next 6 months: [list relevant holidays/events]. For each, include: a campaign name, the promotional offer, email subject line, social media caption, and a creative twist that goes beyond generic 'holiday sale' messaging."

## SECTION 2: SALES (15 Prompts)

**21. Sales Page Copy**
> "Write a long-form sales page for my [product/service] priced at [price]. Target audience: [audience]. Their biggest pain point is [pain]. Include: a headline formula, problem agitation, solution introduction, features-to-benefits breakdown, social proof sections, FAQ section (with objection handling), pricing presentation, guarantee, and urgency-based CTA. Use proven copywriting frameworks."

**22. Cold Outreach Script**
> "Write a cold outreach script for reaching [target: business owners, HR managers, etc.] about my [product/service]. Create versions for: LinkedIn DM (under 100 words), email (under 150 words), and phone call (30-second opening). Each should: lead with value not pitch, reference a specific pain point, include a soft CTA, and feel personal not template-y."

**23. Objection Handling Playbook**
> "I sell [product/service] at [price] to [audience]. The top objections I hear are: 'It's too expensive,' 'I need to think about it,' 'I'm already using [competitor],' 'Now isn't a good time,' and 'I need to talk to my [partner/boss].' For each objection, give me: 3 different response scripts, the psychology behind the objection, and a reframe that leads back to the sale."

**24. Upsell & Cross-Sell Strategy**
> "My main product/service is [product] at [price]. My other offerings are [list them]. Design an upsell and cross-sell strategy including: what to offer at checkout, a post-purchase upsell sequence (3 emails), bundle options with pricing, and scripts for suggesting upgrades during conversations. The goal is to increase average order value by 30%."

**25. Sales Follow-Up Sequence**
> "Create a 7-touch sales follow-up sequence for leads who showed interest in my [product/service] but didn't buy. For each touchpoint, specify: timing, channel (email/text/call), the message, and the psychological trigger being used. Don't be annoying — make each follow-up add genuine value while keeping the sale alive."

**26. Discovery Call Script**
> "Write a discovery call script for selling my [service] to [target audience]. Include: an opening that builds rapport (2 minutes), qualification questions to assess fit (5 minutes), pain-discovery questions that make them feel understood (10 minutes), my solution presentation framework (5 minutes), pricing presentation, and closing. Mark where to pause and listen."

**27. Proposal Follow-Up Emails**
> "I sent a proposal for [service] to a prospect [number] days ago. Write 5 follow-up emails I can send over the next 3 weeks, each with a different angle: (1) checking in, (2) adding value with a relevant insight, (3) social proof/case study, (4) addressing likely hesitation, (5) final 'closing the file' email that creates urgency."

**28. Pricing Page Optimization**
> "Rewrite my pricing page. I offer [list your packages/prices]. Help me: name each tier memorably, write benefit-focused descriptions for each, highlight the best-value option, add strategic anchor pricing, write FAQ copy that overcomes price objections, and suggest a money-back guarantee. Make people feel confident choosing the middle or top tier."

**29. Client Onboarding Welcome Sequence**
> "Design a client onboarding sequence for my [service business]. After someone pays, create: a welcome email with next steps, a client questionnaire (10 key questions), a 'what to expect' guide, a first-week check-in email, and a 30-day check-in. The goal is to make them feel like they made the best decision and reduce buyer's remorse."

**30. Partnership Pitch Template**
> "Write a partnership pitch for approaching [type of complementary business] about a referral or collaboration arrangement. My business is [your business] and their audience would benefit because [reason]. Include: the outreach email, a one-page partnership overview, suggested collaboration structures, and revenue-sharing models."

**31. Sales Metrics Dashboard**
> "Help me set up a sales tracking system. My business is [type] with [revenue range] in monthly revenue. Create: the 10 most important sales KPIs I should track, formulas for calculating each, a weekly sales review checklist, benchmarks for my industry, and red flags that indicate a problem in my sales process."

**32. Webinar Sales Presentation**
> "Outline a 45-minute webinar that sells my [product/service] at [price]. Include: a title that drives registrations, the opening hook (5 min), 3 teaching sections that build toward the sale (25 min), the transition to pitch (2 min), the offer presentation (10 min), and Q&A framework (3 min). The ratio should be 70% value, 30% pitch."

**33. Downsell Strategy**
> "My main offer is [product/service] at [price] and my conversion rate is [percentage]. Design a downsell strategy for people who don't buy. Include: a lower-priced alternative offer, the downsell page copy, an email sequence that presents the downsell, and positioning that makes the downsell feel like a genuine option rather than a consolation prize."

**34. Annual Client Review Template**
> "Create a template for annual client reviews for my [service business]. Include: a results summary section, ROI calculation, recommendations for next year, upsell opportunities presented as solutions, renewal terms, and a satisfaction survey. Make it professional enough to justify a rate increase and strengthen the relationship."

**35. Lost Deal Win-Back Campaign**
> "Design a campaign to win back leads who went cold or chose a competitor. My business is [type] and the main reasons people don't buy are [reasons]. Create: a 5-email re-engagement sequence, a special 'come back' offer, a survey to understand why they left, and scripts for a phone call reconnection. Time this for [weeks/months] after they went cold."

## SECTION 3: CUSTOMER SERVICE (15 Prompts)

**36. FAQ Page Generator**

Make each professional but warm."

**38. Negative Review Response Guide**
> "Write response templates for negative reviews of my [type of business]. Create responses for: a 1-star review with valid complaints, a 1-star review that's unfair/inaccurate, a 3-star 'it was okay' review, a complaint about price, and a complaint about customer service. Each response should: acknowledge the issue, take responsibility where appropriate, offer a solution, and take the conversation offline. Never be defensive."

**39. Chatbot Conversation Flows**
> "Design chatbot conversation flows for my [type of business] website. Create scripts for 5 scenarios: (1) new visitor wants to learn about products/services, (2) existing customer needs support, (3) someone wants pricing information, (4) someone wants to book a call/appointment, (5) someone has a complaint. Include branching logic and escalation points to human support."

**40. Customer Feedback Survey**
> "Create a post-purchase survey for my [type of business] that I can send [time after purchase]. Include 10 questions that measure: overall satisfaction, specific product/service quality, customer effort score, likelihood to recommend (NPS), and open-ended improvement suggestions. Make it completable in under 3 minutes. Include the email that delivers the survey."

**41. Returns & Refund Policy**
> "Write a returns and refund policy for my [type of business]. Make it clear, fair, and customer-friendly while protecting my business. Include: time limits, condition requirements, process steps, exceptions, digital product policies, and how refunds are processed. Write it in plain English (not legalese) and in a tone that makes customers feel safe buying."

**42. Customer Apology Templates**
> "Write 5 apology templates for different severity levels of customer service failures at my [type of business]: (1) minor inconvenience (slight delay), (2) moderate issue (wrong item sent), (3) major problem (service failure), (4) repeated issues (multiple problems with same customer), (5) public/social media complaint. **43. VIP Customer Program**
> "Design a VIP/loyalty program for my [type of business]. Average customer spends [amount] per [timeframe]. Create: tier structure (3 levels), benefits for each tier, point/reward system, exclusive perks, communication plan for VIP members, and the emails to announce the program. Make it drive repeat purchases without cutting into margins too much."

**44. Shipping & Delivery Communication**
> "Create a full shipping communication sequence for my [e-commerce/product] business: (1) order confirmation email, (2) processing/preparing update, (3) shipped notification with tracking, (4) out for delivery alert, (5) delivered confirmation, (6) 'how's your order?' follow-up, (7) delayed shipping apology template. Make each on-brand and include opportunities to build excitement."

**45. Knowledge Base Structure**
> "Help me plan a customer knowledge base / help center for my [type of business]. Create: a category structure (6-8 main categories), 5 article titles per category, an outline for the 3 most important articles, a troubleshooting flowchart for common issues, and a template I can use for all future articles. Prioritize the content that will reduce support tickets the most."

**46. Customer Win-Back Email Series**
> "Create a 4-email win-back series for customers of my [type of business] who haven't purchased in [timeframe]. Email 1: 'We miss you' with no offer. Email 2: 'Here's what's new' with product updates. Email 3: 'Exclusive comeback offer' with discount. Email 4: 'Last chance' with urgency. Include subject lines, preview text, and full body copy."

**47. Escalation Procedure**
> "Create a customer service escalation procedure for my [type of business] with [number] team members. Define: Level 1 (frontline — what they can resolve and how), Level 2 (manager — what triggers escalation), Level 3 (owner — critical situations). Include response time standards, compensation authority at each level, documentation requirements, and scripts for transferring between levels."

**48. Social Media Customer Service Guidelines**
> "Write social media customer service guidelines for my [type of business]. Cover: response time expectations per platform, tone of voice for public responses, when to take conversations to DMs, templates for common public complaints, how to handle trolls vs. legitimate complaints, and a crisis communication protocol for when something goes viral for the wrong reasons."

**49. Client Check-In Templates**
> "Create 6 client check-in email templates for my [service business] to send at key milestones: (1) 1 week after starting, (2) project midpoint, (3) before a major deliverable, (4) after a major deliverable, (5) project completion, (6) 90 days post-completion. Each should gather feedback, strengthen the relationship, and plant seeds for future work."

**50. Customer Service Training Guide**
> "Create a customer service training outline for a new hire at my [type of business]. Include: our service philosophy, the top 20 scenarios they'll encounter (with ideal responses), empathy phrases to use, phrases to never say, de-escalation techniques, when to offer discounts/refunds (decision tree), and a 2-week onboarding schedule."
