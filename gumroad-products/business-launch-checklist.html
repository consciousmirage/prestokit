Phase 1: Legal & Business Structure

Lay the legal foundation first
Weeks 1-2
Business Entity
Choose your business structure (sole proprietor, LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp)LLC is most common for small businesses — protects personal assets
Register your business name with your state's Secretary of StateCheck availability at your state's SOS website before filing
File Articles of Organization (for LLC) or Articles of IncorporationTypical cost: $50-$500 depending on state
Get your EIN (Employer Identification Number) from the IRSFree at IRS.gov — takes 5 minutes online
Draft an Operating Agreement (LLC) or Corporate Bylaws
Register as a foreign entity if operating in multiple states
Licenses & Permits
Research required federal licenses for your industrySBA.gov has a license/permit lookup tool by state and industry
Get state business license(s)
Get local/city business licenseContact your city clerk's office or check the city website
Apply for professional/occupational licenses if required
Register for a sales tax permit if selling taxable goods/services
Obtain zoning permits if operating from a physical location
Get a home occupation permit if running business from home
Legal Protection
File for trademark on your business name and logoSearch USPTO.gov first to ensure your name isn't taken
Create standard client/customer contracts or Terms of Service
Draft a Privacy Policy (required if you collect any customer data)
Create a Terms & Conditions page for your website
Get general liability insuranceEven online businesses should have E&O (Errors & Omissions) insurance
Get professional liability insurance if offering services
Set up a registered agent if required in your state

Phase 2: Finances & Accounting

Set up your money systems
Weeks 2-3
Banking
Open a business checking accountNever mix personal and business finances — this protects your LLC status
Open a business savings account (for taxes and emergency fund)
Get a business credit cardBuilds business credit and earns rewards on business expenses
Set up business PayPal or Stripe account for online payments
Apply for a business line of credit (even if you don't need it yet)
Accounting & Bookkeeping
Choose accounting software (QuickBooks, Wave, FreshBooks, Xero)Wave is free and great for startups; QuickBooks is the industry standard
Set up chart of accounts (income categories, expense categories)
Create invoice templates with your branding
Set up expense tracking system (receipt scanning, categorization)
Establish a bookkeeping routine (weekly or monthly)Set a recurring calendar reminder — don't let it pile up
Find a CPA or tax professionalDo this now, not at tax time — a good CPA saves you money all year
Pricing & Revenue
Research competitor pricing for benchmarking
Calculate your costs (fixed costs, variable costs, overhead)
Set your pricing strategy (cost-plus, value-based, competitive)
Create your pricing page / rate sheet
Determine your break-even point
Set quarterly tax payment reminders (15th of Apr, Jun, Sep, Jan)
Create a simple financial forecast (12-month revenue and expense projections)

Phase 3: Brand & Online Presence

Build your brand and get online
Weeks 3-5
Brand Identity
Finalize your business name (check domain, trademark, and social handle availability)
Write your mission statement (1-2 sentences)
Define your brand values (3-5 core values)
Identify your target audience (create 1-2 customer personas)
Define your brand voice and tone
Write your elevator pitch (30-second version)Template: "I help [audience] achieve [outcome] through [method]"
Write your brand story (the why behind your business)
Visual Identity
Design your logo (primary, secondary, and icon versions)Canva, Fiverr, or 99designs are affordable options
Choose your brand color palette (primary, secondary, accent colors)
Select brand fonts (heading font + body font)
Create a brand style guide (even a simple 1-pager helps)
Design business cards
Create branded templates (email signature, letterhead, social media templates)
Get professional headshots / brand photography
Website
Purchase your domain nameGet .com if possible. Also grab common misspellings
Choose your website platform (WordPress, Squarespace, Shopify, Wix)
Set up web hosting (if not included with your platform)
Build your homepage with clear value proposition
Create your About page (your story, credentials, photos)
Create Services/Products page(s) with pricing
Create Contact page with contact form
Set up Google Analytics for traffic tracking
Set up SSL certificate (https — usually free with hosting)
Make sure site is mobile-responsive
Add Privacy Policy and Terms of Service pages
Set up your business email (you@yourdomain.com)Google Workspace ($6/mo) or Zoho Mail (free) are great options
Social Media
Claim your business name on all major platforms (even if you won't use them all)
Set up Instagram business profile with optimized bio
Set up Facebook business page
Set up LinkedIn company page (and optimize personal profile)
Set up TikTok business account (if relevant)
Choose 2-3 primary platforms to focus onBetter to be great on 2 platforms than mediocre on 5
Create a link-in-bio page (Linktree, Stan Store, or custom)

Phase 4: Marketing & Customer Acquisition

Get your first customers
Weeks 5-7
Marketing Strategy
Define your unique selling proposition (what makes you different)
Research your competitors (pricing, messaging, channels, strengths, weaknesses)
Identify your top 3 marketing channelsGo where your customers already are. Don't spread too thin
Set marketing goals (specific, measurable, time-bound)
Determine your marketing budget (even if it's $0 to start)
Create a marketing calendar for your first 90 days
Content Marketing
Define your content pillars (3-5 topic categories you'll create content about)
Create a content calendar (what to post, where, and when)
Write 3-5 blog posts before launch for SEOTarget "how to" and "what is" keywords related to your business
Create 2 weeks of social media content in advance
Create a lead magnet (free download, quiz, checklist, etc.)
Set up your email marketing platform (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)
Create a welcome email sequence (3-5 emails for new subscribers)
Create opt-in forms on your website for email capture
Local & SEO Marketing
Set up Google Business Profile (critical for local businesses)This is free and makes you appear in Google Maps results
Get listed on Yelp
List on industry-specific directories
Do basic SEO optimization on all website pages (title tags, meta descriptions, headers)
Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console
Research 20-30 target keywords for your business
Paid Advertising
Set up Facebook/Instagram Ads Manager
Install Facebook Pixel on your website
Set up Google Ads account
Plan a small test ad budget ($5-10/day to start)Start with one platform, one campaign. Don't spread budget thin
Create ad creatives (images, copy, headlines)

Phase 5: Operations & Systems

Build the machine that runs your business
Weeks 6-8
Tools & Software
Set up project management tool (Notion, Asana, Trello, ClickUp)
Set up CRM (customer relationship management) — even a spreadsheet to startHubSpot CRM is free and powerful enough for most small businesses
Set up scheduling tool (Calendly, Acuity) for meetings/calls
Set up cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox) for file organization
Set up communication tools (Slack, Google Workspace)
Set up password manager (LastPass, 1Password) for security
Set up backup system for all important files and data
Processes & SOPs
Document your sales process (inquiry → proposal → close → onboard)
Document your service delivery process or order fulfillment
Create a client onboarding checklist
Create a client offboarding process
Set up a customer feedback collection system
Create templates for proposals, contracts, and invoices
Document your daily/weekly business routines
Team (If Applicable)
Identify roles you need to fill (now vs. later)
Write job descriptions for roles you'll hire for
Set up payroll system (Gusto, ADP) if hiring employees
Create an employee handbook (even if just a few pages)
Set up contractor agreement templates if using freelancersAlways get a signed contract before work begins

Phase 6: Pre-Launch & Launch Day

Go time — open for business
Week 8+
Pre-Launch (1-2 Weeks Before)
Test your entire purchase/booking flow from start to finish
Proofread all website copy
Test all website forms, links, and buttons
Test your payment processing (run a test transaction)
Test your email sequences (subscribe to your own list)
Create a launch announcement post / email
Tell friends and family about your launch
Set up analytics tracking to measure launch performance
Prepare a launch-day social media schedule
Launch Day
Publish your launch announcement across all channels
Send launch email to your list
Post on all social media platforms
Personally message 20+ people who might be interested or might share
Monitor everything closely (website uptime, payment processing, emails)
Respond quickly to all inquiries
Post-Launch (First 30 Days)
Collect feedback from your first customers
Ask for reviews and testimonials
Review your analytics — what's working, what's not
Adjust your marketing based on early data
Set up a referral ask from happy customers
Establish your ongoing weekly routine
Celebrate your launch! You did it.Seriously — take a moment to acknowledge what you've built
